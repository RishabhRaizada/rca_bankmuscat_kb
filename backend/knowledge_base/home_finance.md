# Home Finance — Knowledge Base

## Overview
Bank Muscat provides home financing (both conventional and Islamic via Meethaq) for property purchase, construction, and renovation. Home Finance is a high-value, document-intensive product with multiple stages: application → approval → disbursement → repayment → settlement. Complaints typically arise around delays in processing, document handling, and settlement certificates. Loan = conventional; Finance = Islamic (Meethaq) — same operational processes, different terminology.

## Key Documents in Home Finance
- **Title Deed (Mulkiya)**: Property ownership document held by Bank Muscat as collateral during the finance period. Released after full settlement.
- **Krooki**: Site plan/property sketch. Held by bank during finance.
- **Contractor Agreement**: Construction agreement document. Required for construction home finance.
- **Insurance**: Property/life insurance document held by bank.
- **Valuation Report**: Independent property valuation. Required for finance approval.
- **NOC (No Objection Certificate)**: Certificate for specific requests (e.g., selling the property with active finance). SLA: **5 working days**.
- **CLC (Clearance Letter Certificate)**: Certificate confirming full loan/finance settlement. SLA: **5 working days**.

## Common Issues & Root Causes

### Delay in Cancelling Finance/Loan Application
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Cancellation Fees Not Paid, Customer Retraction, Disbursement Completed, Incomplete Documents, System Error.
- **Resolution**: Contact The Customer (to confirm intent and payment status) → Request Customer For Missing Documents → Work In Progress (if processing) → Rectify.
- **Note**: If disbursement is already completed, cancellation is significantly more complex and requires Back Office coordination.

### Delay in Loan/Finance Amendment Processing
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Branch Delay, Customer Request, Customer Retraction, Incomplete Documents, Invalid Documents, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly → Escalation To Concerned Stakeholder → Rectify.

### Loan Settlement Not Done / Delay in Loan Settlement (Partial or Full)
- **What it is**: Customer has paid or arranged to pay off their loan/finance but settlement has not been processed.
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Branch Delay, Human Error, Incomplete Documents, Operational Issue.
- **Known pattern**: Settlement delays are significant because they prevent release of title deed and CLC, blocking property transactions.
- **Resolution**: Escalation To Concerned Stakeholder → Follow Up Initiated → Advise Customer Of Missing Documents. Expedite processing to avoid further delays.

### NOC Not Released (More Than 5 Working Days)
- **What it is**: Customer applied for NOC but it hasn't been issued beyond the 5-working-day SLA.
- **Root Cause**: Branch Delay, Customer Request (incomplete application), Incomplete Documents, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Inform The Customer About Incomplete Details Or Reason For Rejection (e.g., active credit card or unpaid liabilities). Branch must check View Remarks in system and do the needful accordingly.
- **Note**: NOC can be rejected if customer has an active credit card linked to the property or has unpaid liabilities. This must be communicated clearly.

### CLC Not Released (More Than 5 Working Days)
- **Root Cause**: Customer Request, Incomplete Documents, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Inform The Customer About Incomplete Details. Branch submits the documents. Branch must check View Remarks and do the needful.

### Title Deed (Mulkiya) / Krooki / Insurance / Valuation Not Released
- **What it is**: Customer has fully settled the finance but the bank hasn't released the collateral documents.
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Customer Request, Invalid Documents, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer For Missing Documents → Process The Request.

### Delay in Stage Payment Release
- **What it is**: For construction finance, payments to the contractor are released in stages upon completion milestones. Delay in releasing a stage payment holds up construction.
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Customer Request, Incomplete Documents.
- **Resolution**: Ask Customer To Submit Application → Check Document And Rectify → Escalation To Concerned Stakeholder.

### Delay in Loan/Finance Deferment
- **What it is**: Customer requested deferment (postponement) of loan installments but it hasn't been processed.
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Customer Request, Incomplete Documents.
- **Resolution**: Ask Customer To Submit Application → Check Document And Rectify → Escalation To Concerned Stakeholder.

### Finance Waiver Request Not Done
- **Root Cause**: Back Office Delay, Customer Request, Incomplete Documents.
- **Resolution**: Ask Customer To Submit Application → Check Document And Rectify → Escalation To Concerned Stakeholder.

## Key Operational Facts
- **NOC SLA**: 5 working days. Beyond this = complaint-worthy delay.
- **CLC SLA**: 5 working days. Beyond this = complaint-worthy delay.
- **Settlement documents**: After full settlement, all collateral documents (Mulkiya, Krooki, Insurance, Valuation) must be returned to customer. Each has its own processing timeline.
- **CAD (Credit Administration Department)**: Primary department handling settlement, NOC/CLC issuance, and collateral release. Delays in these processes route to CAD.
- **Back Office vs Branch**: Applications are processed at branch; complex decisioning, disbursement, and settlement are handled by Back Office/CAD.
- **"View Remarks"**: System field where Back Office records reasons for rejection or pending requirements — always check before informing customer.
- **Islamic finance terminology**: "Finance" = Loan; "Profit" = Interest; "Financing" = Lending. Meethaq products use Islamic finance structures (Murabaha for purchase, Ijarah for leasing).
