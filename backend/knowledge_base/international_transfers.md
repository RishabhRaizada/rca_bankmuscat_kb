# International Transfers — Knowledge Base

## Overview
This category covers outgoing and incoming international fund transfers via SWIFT, and inquiry-based complaints about transfer fees, available destinations, and processing times. Bank Muscat SWIFT code: **BKMOOMRX**.

## Transfer Types
- **SWIFT International**: Transfer to any SWIFT-connected bank globally. Requires beneficiary's IBAN/account number and bank's BIC/SWIFT code.
- **Speed Transfer**: Fast remittance to India, Sri Lanka, Philippines, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Egypt. RO 1.5 per transaction (Egypt: free). Available via mBanking.
- **Within GCC**: Covered under GCC Bank Devices category if ATM-related; international transfers if bank-to-bank.

## Transfer Charges (mBanking)
- International SWIFT: RO 15 or RO 5 (varies by amount/destination — exact current rates should be verified on bankmuscat.com)
- Speed Transfer India (NEFT): RO 1.5; IMPS (ICICI/HDFC): RO 1.5; IMPS other: RO 2.0
- Speed Transfer Philippines/Sri Lanka/Bangladesh/Pakistan: RO 1.5
- Speed Transfer Egypt: Free
- VAT at 5% applied on transaction charges.

## Processing SLAs
- **Non-Eastern currencies** (USD, EUR, GBP, etc.): Same day if submitted before 14:00 Oman time.
- **Eastern currencies** (AUD, SGD, JPY, NZD, HKD): Next working day.
- **Speed Transfer India**: Same day per RBI guidelines.
- **Speed Transfer Philippines**: Instant for BDO Unibank; same day before 12:00 for others.
- **Speed Transfer Sri Lanka**: Instant for Commercial Bank of Ceylon; same day before 12:00 for others.
- **Speed Transfer Egypt**: Instant for Bank Misr; other banks 10am–4pm Oman time Sun–Thu.

## Common Issues & Root Causes

### Enquiry on SWIFT Charges
- **What it is**: Customer asks about commission/fees for international SWIFT transfers (e.g., to US banks). Often asked via social media because fee information is not easily found on the website.
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue — inadequate publication of fee information on digital channels.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly with current SWIFT fee schedule.

### Enquiry on Countries Available for Fund Transfer
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue — information not readily available on digital channels.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly.

### Enquiry on Overseas Transfer Limit
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly. Daily limit for international transfers: RO 5,000 (3 transactions/day via mBanking).

### Transfer Not Received by Beneficiary (Non-Receipt of Funds)
- **Root Cause**: Human Error (wrong beneficiary details), System Error (SWIFT processing failure), Customer Request (inquiry on status).
- **Resolution path**: Check the transaction in T24 → contact customer for additional information if needed → contact the other bank → initiate recall or beneficiary correction request if wrong details were used.

### Duplicate Amount Transferred
- **Root Cause**: Human Error (double submission), System Error (duplicate processing), Customer Request.
- **Resolution**: Check The Details And Rectify; check T24; contact other bank; initiate recall if needed.

### Wrong Exchange Rate Applied
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue, System Error, Customer Request.
- **Resolution**: Check Document And Rectify.

## Key Operational Facts
- SWIFT information (Bank Muscat SWIFT code BKMOOMRX) is documented on the website but not prominently — a known gap causing customer inquiries.
- All complaints about GCC ATM cash failures should be in "GCC Bank Devices" category, not here.
- For recalls: Bank Muscat can initiate a SWIFT recall message; success depends on whether the beneficiary bank has already credited the funds.
- "Send Beneficiary Correction Request To Other Bank" = formal SWIFT message correcting wrong beneficiary details.
- "Send Cancellation Request To Other Bank" = formal SWIFT cancellation of a transfer.
