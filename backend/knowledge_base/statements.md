# Statements — Knowledge Base

## Overview
Bank Muscat provides account statements via multiple channels: printed (at branch), e-statement (email), and digital (mBanking/internet banking). This category covers failures in receiving statements, inability to open e-statements, and incorrect narrations in statements.

## Statement Types & Channels
- **Mini Statement**: Last 25 transactions. Available via ATM or mBanking.
- **Digital Statement (mBanking)**: Up to 3 months of transactions, downloadable/emailable. Available via mBanking app.
- **Printed Account Statement**: Requested at branch or via internet banking. Can be emailed directly from the system.
- **E-Statement**: Periodic statement delivered automatically to customer's registered email.
- **Archived Statement**: Statements older than 3 months. Requires escalation to IT team — not available via standard mBanking or internet banking.
- **Detailed Statement**: Full transaction history for a specified period.

## Common Issues & Root Causes

### Printed Account Statement Not Received
- **Root Cause**: Branch Delay (statement not processed by branch staff), Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Escalate To IT Team If Statement Required Is Archived (for old statements). Otherwise process standard statement at branch level.

### E-Statement Not Received
- **Root Cause**: Customer Request (email address wrong or not set up), Operational Issue (auto-send not configured), Staff Delay.
- **Resolution**: Send The Statement → Update The Email ID if address is incorrect.

### Unable to Open E-Statement
- **What it is**: Customer receives the statement email but cannot open/decrypt the attached PDF.
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue — incorrect password or corrupted file.
- **Resolution**: Escalate To Account Operation Team If ID Is Wrong. E-statements are typically password-protected (password = customer ID or date of birth).

### Incorrect / Missing Narration in Archived Statement
- **What it is**: Transaction descriptions in archived statements (>3 months old) are incorrect or blank.
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue or System Error — data migration issues when statements moved to archival storage.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly; Rectify Technical Error.

### Incorrect / Missing Narration in Detailed Statement
- **Root Cause**: Operational Issue or System Error — narration not captured correctly at transaction time.
- **Resolution**: Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly; Rectify Technical Error.

### Request for Further Transaction Details
- **What it is**: Customer needs more detail about a specific transaction than what appears on their statement.
- **Root Cause**: Various — Back Office Delay, Branch Delay, Customer Request, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution path**: Check T24 → Check The Customer Account And Provide The Necessary Details → Check The Transaction. For international transfers, check with the other bank.

### Request for Archived Transaction Details / Teller Deposit Details
- **What it is**: Customer wants records of transactions older than what is available on mBanking (>3 months) or specific teller deposit records.
- **Root Cause**: Customer Request, Operational Issue.
- **Resolution**: Check The Customer Account And Provide The Necessary Details.

## Key Operational Facts
- **3-month limit in mBanking**: The mobile app only shows 3 months of transactions. Anything older requires a formal statement request processed at branch or via internet banking.
- **Archived statements** (>3 months): Must be escalated to the IT team. Branch staff cannot retrieve these directly.
- **E-statement password**: Typically the customer's Bank Muscat customer ID or a combination of date of birth — commonly the cause of "unable to open" complaints.
- **Enquiry on Account Statement**: Informational only. Contact Customer And Inform Accordingly about available statement channels and how to obtain them.
- Staff delays in processing statement requests are a known issue — customer is often told to come back or wait.
