# Queue System — Knowledge Base

## Overview
This category covers customer complaints about branch queue management, including excessively long wait times, queue jumping, and unfair service ordering. These are branch-level operational complaints.

## Queue Types
- **Electronic token system**: Customers take a numbered token on entry. Displayed on screens when number is called.
- **Service windows**: Different queues for different service types (teller, customer service, loans, etc.).

## Common Issues & Root Causes

### Long Queue / Excessive Wait Times
- **What it is**: Customer waits an unacceptably long time (3+ hours reported) for routine banking services.
- **Root Cause**: Staff Delay (processing time per customer is too long) or Operational Issue (understaffing relative to customer volume).
- **Known pattern**: 3-hour wait times at Bank Muscat branches have been reported publicly, causing significant reputational damage. Customers reference the bank's "Number 1" positioning as undermined by such service failures.
- **Resolution**: Contact The Customer To Understand The Issue; Apologise And Rectify; escalate to branch manager; review staffing allocation.

### Queue Jumping / Allowed Another Customer to Jump the Queue
- **What it is**: A customer who arrived later was served before customers who had been waiting longer. Staff bypassed the formal queue sequence.
- **Root Cause**: Staff Delay — staff exercised unauthorised discretion to prioritise a customer, or failed to follow queue sequencing protocols.
- **Known pattern**: In one documented case, a customer waited nearly 3 hours while another who arrived 20 minutes prior was served first. This suggests staff are responding to social pressure or personal discretion without manager oversight.
- **Resolution**: Apologise And Rectify; Contact The Customer To Understand The Issue; formal investigation; refresher training on queue protocols.

## Key Operational Facts
- **Acceptable wait time**: Should not exceed 30 minutes for routine transactions. Wait times of 3 hours are critical failures.
- **Queue exception policy**: Any deviation from token order requires manager approval. Staff should never exercise personal discretion to bypass queue sequence.
- **Branch manager responsibility**: Branch managers are responsible for monitoring queue performance and deploying additional resources during peak periods.
- **Customer impact**: Long queues and queue jumping create public social media complaints, amplifying reputational damage.
- **Remedy**: Contact customer personally, apologise formally, conduct incident investigation, provide refresher training to all branch staff.
